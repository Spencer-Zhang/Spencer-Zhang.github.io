Affirmation and Stereotype Threat

June 8, 2014

Back in week 1, I identified my three most important values as Knowledge, Challenge, and Growth. Thinking about these values helps give me an accurate perspective on where I am right now. I think about how much more I have to learn, and how many more challenges I have to face, but I also think about how far I've come. The values also help me remember that facing frustration is a natural part of learning. If I'm having trouble with a programming problem, it doesn't mean I'm stupid. It just means I haven't learned the tricks that more experienced programmers have learned already. As soon as I've learned these techniques (and it could be as early as the very next day), I would look back at the problem and it would look perfectly plain and easy.

Whenever I'm feeling down, it helps me to fall back on my hobbies, such as drawing or playing video games. It's important for me to do something non-competitive, where I wouldn't have to compare myself to other people.
